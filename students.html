Student Research Showcase

Call for Submissions

CERTAIN 2026 invites submissions to its Student Research Showcase. The showcase is designed as a non-archival two-page abstract track. We welcome work in progress and encourage submissions that would benefit from community feedback.

  1. 01

    Categories

    Who can submit

    • Undergraduate
    • Master's
    • PhD

    High school students may submit in the undergraduate category. Participants outside academia seeking PhD positions may submit in the master's category.

  2. 02

    Awards

    Each category winner will receive an award and an oral presentation slot. Oral presenters will also be offered an opt-in mentoring round with a senior PC member to strengthen their slides and delivery.

  3. 03

    Requirements

    What to submit:

    • A non-archival two-page abstract
    • A completed CERTAIN Checklist

    For multi-author projects, submissions must list all contributors and clarify the participant's own contribution and the contribution of the full team.

  4. 04

    Timeline

    Submission deadline: TBA

    Author notification: TBA

    Further review dates will be posted with the full call for submissions.

  5. 05

    Visa Support

    Authors may request an early poster decision to begin the visa process, while oral selections will follow the standard timeline. This track will have an earlier deadline, require a short statement of need, and the number of submissions in the track will be capped so that decisions can be returned within two weeks.

  6. 06

    Submission Link

    The submission link will be provided upon acceptance by the NeurIPS Workshop Committee.

Note: Submissions will be non-archival, so students can receive feedback without blocking future conference or journal submissions.

Scope

CERTAIN sits at the intersection of AI and formal methods. It is not a general workshop on AI safety, privacy, or ethics. We include those topics only when they rest on specifications, proofs, certificates, or formal analysis.

  1. 01

    Formal Methods for AI

    The specification, analysis, and verification of AI-enabled systems.

  2. 02

    AI for Formal Methods

    The use of machine learning to support reasoning, synthesis, and verification with formal guarantees.

Topics in Scope

  • Formal specifications for systems with learning components
  • Safety and liveness properties for AI-enabled systems
  • Formal verification of machine learning models
  • Formal analysis of AI-enabled cyber-physical systems
  • Interpretability, robustness, and privacy with formal guarantees
  • Neuro-symbolic and proof-backed trustworthy AI
  • Machine learning for formal verification, synthesis, and control
  • Machine learning for automated reasoning and theorem proving
  • Specification-based code generation with AI components
  • Neural proof certificates

Out of Scope

  • General discussions of AI safety, privacy, ethics, or governance without a formal core
  • Purely empirical evaluations with no specification, proof obligation, or formal guarantee
  • Work on AI applications that does not engage with verification, certification, or formal reasoning
